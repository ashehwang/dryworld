Water Seems Abundant.

That is, when you are lucky enough to be living in cities of developed countries. 70% of the Earth's surface is covered with water. Getting water is as easy as turning on the faucet. But that is not all of us.

Ocean water is undrinkable. You'll only get thirstier if you try drink it; its sodium content is too high to satiate our thirst. Only 2% of the water on this planet is drinkable fresh water. And yet, 80% of that fresh water (1.6% of planet's water) is actually is trapped and locked in polar ice caps and glaciers. That leaves only 0.4% of the water on this planet to be shared with 7 billion people, 19.6 billion chickens, 980 million pigs, and 1.4 billion cattle.

Just like fresh air, we don't appreciate how essential they are because most of us who are able to read this have easy access to it.

But in developing world,

The average African woman would walk 4 miles per day carrying 44 pounds to have water.

Women living in 3rd world country would spend a total of 200 millions hour per day collecting water. (Which is equal to the time North American women spend shopping each day.)

1 out of 6 people still don't have access to water sanitation.

2.6 billion people don't have access to portable water.

Water is an essential commodity to be shared among all of inhabitants of this planet, and the future generations beyond us. Little changes can lead to a huge difference. Living with less water, without compromising the quality of life, if very possible with just easy small steps we take. It can be as simple as choosing to eat a chicken sandwich instead of a burger once a week!
